Pride Month Special
As we celebrate Pride Month in June, honoring the resilience, diversity, and triumphs of the LGBTQ+ community, we shine a spotlight on two of the most interesting speakers, who are making significant strides in advocating for equality, acceptance, and understanding. These trailblazers are ready to share their journeys, challenges, and triumphs, offering motivation and hope to countless individuals.
Sushant Divgikar
The Indisputable ‘Drag Queen’ of India & an LGBTQ Activist
Sushant Divgikar, is the first drag artiste from India to ever feature on the ‘Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia List’. He is a renowned personality in the LGBTQ+ community celebrated for his accomplishments as a model, actor, singer, columnist, pageant director, and video jockey. Crowned Mr Gay India 2014, Sushant made history by securing three individual and two group sub-titles.
Sushant has also made notable TV appearances in 'Bigg Boss 8' and 'Sa Re Ga Ma Pa', where he won the golden buzzer. Recognized as one of GQ's 50 Most Influential Young Indians of 2018, Sushant's journey as an openly gay individual and advocate for LGBTQ+ rights resonates deeply, making him a compelling speaker with a widespread appeal.

Gauri Sawant
A Pioneering Transgender Activist instrumental in fighting for the rights of the transgender community in India. | India’s First Transgender mother
Gauri Sawant, a courageous transgender activist, played a pivotal role in securing Aadhaar cards for the third gender following the NALSA judgment in 2014, a landmark ruling that recognised transgender people as a third gender in India. In 2019, the Election Commission of India (EC) designated Gauri Sawant as one of 12 election ambassadors from Maharashtra, the first member from the LGBTQIA+ community to hold the position. Gauri gained fame by adopting the daughter of a deceased sex worker, challenging societal norms as India's first transgender mother.
In 2000 she founded 'Sakhi Char Chowghi,' an NGO offering counseling and promoting safe sex in Mumbai suburbs. Gauri also featured in a heartwarming Vicks Vaporub advertisement with her daughter that went viral on social media. Gauri's inspiring journey was brought to the screen in the biopic Taali, starring Sushmita Sen, which was released on JioCinema last year.
